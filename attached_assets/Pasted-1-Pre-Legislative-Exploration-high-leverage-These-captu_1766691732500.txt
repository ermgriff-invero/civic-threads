1. Pre-Legislative / Exploration (high leverage)

These capture thinking before an ordinance or resolution exists — huge for institutional memory.

🧭 Policy Concept

Early-stage idea or proposal under consideration
Use when: “We’re exploring X, no formal language yet”
Why it matters: Prevents ideas from living only in email + memory

🔍 Research Memo

Background research, peer city analysis, data review
Use when: Staff is gathering evidence
Differentiator: Enables anonymized benchmarking later

💬 Public Input Summary

Community feedback, surveys, hearings, workshops
Use when: Translating raw public sentiment into policy signal
Huge trust & transparency win

2. Legislative / Formal Actions (you already have most)

You already cover these well, but one addition is important:

🧾 Staff Report ⭐️

Official recommendation from staff to council/board
Why it’s critical:

This is often the real decision document

Where rationale, risks, and tradeoffs live

Cities rely on these constantly

If you add only one new option, make it this.

3. Execution & Operations (massively underserved)

Most civic tools stop at passage. Cities don’t.

⚙️ Implementation Plan

How an adopted policy will actually be executed
Use when: Assigning owners, timelines, departments
This is where policy succeeds or dies

📊 Impact Assessment

Fiscal, equity, environmental, or operational impacts
Use when: Evaluating second-order effects
Sets you up perfectly for outcome learning

📈 Performance Review

Post-implementation results vs expectations
Use when: “Did this actually work?”
This unlocks long-term outcome intelligence

4. Legal & Compliance (often overlooked)
⚖️ Legal Opinion

Counsel interpretation, risk assessment, or compliance view
Use when: There’s ambiguity or legal exposure
Keeps legal reasoning attached to decisions

📌 Administrative Rule / Policy

Internal policy or rule not passed by council
Common in: Zoning, permitting, HR, enforcement
Often invisible but hugely impactful

5. Meta / Process (power-user features)

These make Civic Threads feel intelligent, not just archival.

🧵 Decision Log

Record of key decisions, alternatives considered, and why
Use when: Capturing rationale across meetings
Prevents “why did we do this?” 3 years later

🗓 Meeting Brief

Agenda context, summaries, pre-reads
Use when: Preparing electeds or staff
Lightweight, high-frequency document
